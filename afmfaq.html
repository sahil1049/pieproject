  • Atomic Force Microscope:FAQ


Q 1.What should be the size of sample?

Ans: We can use a circular sample that should not be greater than 203 mm in diameter and have a maximum thickness of 16mm.


Q 2. What is maximum area scan that can be done at a time?

Ans: The maximum area that can be scanned at a time is 90 μm x 90 μm.


Q3. Which software is used for data analysis?

Ans: For data analysis NanoScope Analysis software is used.


Q 4. What are the user specified feedback parameter while operating the software?

Ans: The user specified feedback parameter are Setpoint and gain.


Q 5. What are the modes available in this system for performing an experiment?

Ans: The modes which are available are electrical and magnetic, Mechanical properties, Tapping mode, Contact mode, ScanAsyst and other SPM modes.


Q 6. What are the parameter obtained from cantilever tuning in tapping mode?

Ans: The parameter obtained from cantilever tuning in tapping mode are drive frequency and drive amplitude.


Q 7. Is AFM of powder sample can be done?

Ans: It is difficult to do AFM of dry powder sample. There is more possibility that its particles will move when the tip is doing scanning over it, also it can contaminate the tip.


Q 8. What is tuning of cantilever?

Ans: A range of oscillation frequencies are applied to the cantilever to determine its resonance frequency. This process is known as tuning of cantilever.


Q 9. What is error signal?

Ans: The difference between the setpoint and the actual deflection is referred as error signal.


Q 10. What happen if the applied gain is too low?

Ans: The system will not respond fast enough to the changes in sample topography, the image quality will be poor and the tip will wear quickly.


Q 11. What happen if the applied gain is too high?

Ans: The feedback loop become unstable and the image will look excessively noisy.


Q 12. What type of sample can be analysed using AFM?

Ans: It can be used to analyse conducting as well as insulating samples.


Q 13. How to prepare powder sample for AFM imaging?

Ans: For imaging powder sample either powder can be converted to a pellet or powder is used for making its dilute solution and then dispersing the solution over fresh glass slide/mica, then drying it results the particles get fixed over it.


Q 14. How the feedback loop works?

Ans: The feedback loop involves continually comparing the detector signal to the setpoint. If they are not equal, a voltage is applied to the scanner in order to move the probe either closer to or farther from the sample surface to bring the error signal back to zero. This applied voltage is commonly used as the signal for generating an image.


Q 15. Does it require to clean the sample?

Ans: Yes, AFM is a surface technique so it is very important to clean the sample.


Q 16. What are SPM (Scanning probe microscopy) techniques?

Ans: In SPM techniques a sharp probe is scanned across the surface and tip surface interaction is monitored. The two primary form of it is AFM and STM.


Q 17. What is the difference between STM (Scanning tunnelling microscopy) and AFM?

Ans: STM works by maintaining a constant tunnelling current between the tip and surface. It is used for conducting sample. AFM works by maintaining constant interaction force between the tip and surface. It is used for conducting as well as insulating sample.


Q 18. What is the interaction force between the tip and surface?

Ans: The interaction forces is Vander Waals, electrostatic, covalent, capillary and chemical forces.


Q 19. What are the three primary modes of AFM?

Ans: The three primary modes are contact, non-contact and tapping mode.


Q 21. What are features of laser used in Dimension Icon SPM?

Ans: It contains a diode laser with an output of less than 1.0 mW at 670 nm.


Q 22. Which probe artifacts are observed in AFM?

Ans: Double tip, Tip side wall image.


Q 23. What happens in double tip artifact?

Ans: The probe tip has more than one point in contact with the sample. This most often occurs when the tip breaks and leaves a jagged point. Sometimes double tips are caused by contamination rather than breakage, in which case sometimes the contamination disappears on its own or the probe can be cleaned.


Q 24. What happens in tip side wall image artifact?

Ans: A second tip artifact occurs on very tall samples or samples where the slope of the features is greater than the slope of the tip. This causes the tip sidewall to interact with the sample instead of the tip apex. The typical appearance is that of an almost linear ramp-like artifact around the feature.


Q 25. How many techniques have been proposed to characterize cantilever spring constants?

Ans: These can generally be grouped into three categories: Dimensional models, Static deflection measurements and Dynamic deflection measurements.


Q 26. What is dimensional model?

Ans: Here fully theoretical analysis or semi-empirical formulas are used to calculate the cantilever spring constants based on their dimensions and material properties, eg Estimation from cantilever geometry.


Q 27. What is static deflection measurements?

Ans: In this the spring constant is determined by loading the cantilever with a known static force,eg The added mass method.


Q 28. What is dynamic deflection measurements?

Ans: In this the resonance behaviour of the cantilever is related back to its spring constant, eg rectangular cantilever, measuring thermal noise and hydrodynamic model.


Q 29. What are typical sample substrates?

Ans: Most scanning probe systems are able use the following substrates:
1. Microscope slides
2. Cover slips
3. Mica discs and sheets
4. Petri dishes.


Q 30. What are important steps to adjust laser on photodetector?

Ans: 1) Verify that there is a spot visible on the Dimension Icon scanner filter screen.
2) Verify that you have an appropriate laser sum signal (greater than 0.5 V), displayed in the Dimension Icon LCD or the schematic below the video panel in the Setup view.
3) Center the laser detector signal in the photodetector schematic using the photodetector adjustment knobs located on the left side of the Dimension Icon scanner.


Q 31. How to load and unload a probe in dimension icon scanner?

Ans: To load and unload a probe from the Dimension Icon you will need to use the probe holder stand. The probe holder stand is a 2.5-inch diameter black anodized aluminum cylinder that ships with the Dimension Icon SPM to facilitate installation of the probe substrate onto the Dimension Scanner Probe Holders. The probe holder stand has three stations for different probe holders: Standard AFM Fluid imaging AFM STM


Q 32. What types of image analysis options present?

Ans: Depth, power spectral density, roughness and step.


Q 33. Specify the image modify options that is available in image analysis software?

Ans: Erase, Flatten, low pass filter, median filter and plane fit.


Q 34. What are the two gain parameters that control the scaling of the error signals?

Ans: Proportional gain and Integral gain.


Q 35. What is proportional gain?

Ans: It scales the error signal by a user specified value. The correction applied is proportional to the magnitude of the error signal.


Q 36. What is Integral gain?

Ans: It is proportional to both the magnitude of the error signal as well as the duration of the error signal. Since the integral gain is scaled by the accumulated error over time that should have been corrected already, this term has a very significant effect on the stability of the system.

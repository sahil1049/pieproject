  • PL/Raman Spectroscopy: FAQ

Q 1. Why should I use Raman spectroscopy?

Ans: Raman is a vibrational spectroscopy which is used for identification of molecules.


Q 2. What type of materials exhibit Raman spectra?

Ans: Molecules those undergoes the change in polarizability exhibit Raman scattering.


Q 3. What is the difference in FTIR and Raman?

Ans: FTIR depends on the change in dipole moments whereas Raman scattering is based on change in polarizability of molecule. FTIR shows the absolute frequency absorbed by the molecule but Raman spectra shows the shift in frequency of inelastically scattered light by the molecule.


Q 4. What are the different fields in which Raman spectroscopy is used?

Ans: There are various fields in which Raman spectroscopy is used as characterization tool to study the optical and crystallographic properties of material. Raman spectra is known as a fingerprint of molecule. It has promising application in detection and identification of materials e.g. drugs, narcotics, bacteria, virus, semiconductor 2D materials, Electrical properties of CNTs, chemical composition analysis etc.


Q 5. Can we do Raman mapping on this set up?

Ans: Yes, one can do Raman mapping on this instrument.


Q 6. How long does it take to acquire a Raman spectrum?

Ans: The acquisition time depends on a number of factors, such as the sample itself, the desired spectral quality, and the Raman spectrometer being used. However, typical modern Raman spectrometers can acquire good quality Raman spectra in a few seconds.

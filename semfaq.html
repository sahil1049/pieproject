  • Scanning Electon Microscope(SEM):FAQ

Q 1. What is SEM-EDAX analysis?

Ans: SEM provides detailed high-resolution images of the sample by rastering a focussed electron beam across the surface and detecting secondary or backscattered electron signal. An energy dispersive X-ray analyser (EDAX or EDA) is also used to provide elemental identification and quantitative compositional information..


Q 2. Why SEM test is done?

Ans: The SEM uses a focused beam of high-energy electrons to generate a variety of signals at the surface of solid specimens. The SEM is also capable of performing analyses of selected point locations on the sample.


Q 3. What are the main components of the EDAX system?

Ans: The EDAX system is comprised three main components: (1) X-ray detector- It detects and converts X-ray into electronic signals, (2) Pulse processor- It measures the electronic signals to determine the energy of each X-ray detected and (3) Multiple channel analyser- It displays and interprets the X-ray data.


Q 4. What can SEM be used for?

Ans: A scanning electron microscope (SEM) scans a focused electron beam over a surface to create an image. The electrons in the beam interact with the sample, producing various signals that can be used to obtain information about the surface topography and composition.


Q 5. How do EDAX detectors work?

Ans: EDAX systems are typically integrated with SEM instrument. An EDAX detector contains a crystal that absorbs the energy of incoming x-rays by ionization, yielding free electrons in the crystal that become conductive and produce an electrical charge bias.


Q 6. What is EDAX mapping?

Ans: Energy Dispersive X-Ray Analysis (EDAX), referred to as EDS or EDAX, is an x-ray technique used to identify the elemental composition of materials. The data generated by EDAX analysis consist of spectra showing peaks corresponding to the elements making up the true composition of the sample being analysed. In addition, 3 elements can be displayed simultaneously in Red, Green and Blue (RGB) colour, overlaid upon the backscattered electron image..


Q 7. Is EDAX quantitative?

Ans: In quantitative EDAX microanalysis in SEM, the mass fractions or weight % of the elements present in the sample are calculated. The spectra are processed to remove Bremsstrahlung X-rays and spectral artifacts, and then the characteristic X-rays are compared with data measured from standard reference materials.


Q 8. What is EDAX testing methodology?

Ans: EDAX analysis detects x-rays emitted from the sample during bombardment by the SEM electron beam and characterizes the elemental chemical composition of the analysed volume.


Q 9. What is keV and cps/eV?

Ans: keV: kilo-electron-volt, accelerating voltage range used for EDAX analysis, cps/eV: counts per second per electron-volt.


Q 10. What is the difference between EDAX and WDAX?

Ans: Energy dispersive X-ray spectrometers (EDAX) sort the X-rays based on their energy; while wavelength dispersive X-ray spectrometers (WDAX) sort the X- rays based on their wavelengths. WDAX systems use X-ray diffraction as the means by which they separate X-rays of different wavelengths.


Q 11. What is the maximum size and height of the sample mounted inside the vacuum chamber?

Ans: The maximum size of the sample should be 70 mm as this is the standard feature of the system and the maximum height of the sample should be 50 mm..


Q 12. What are the three observation condition modes are used?

Ans: The three modes are 5kV, 15kV and Analysis modes.

1. 5kV mode – emphasizes surface detail or generally used for soft materials.

2. 15kV mode – can be used throughout the magnification range and gives the best resolution.

3. Analysis mode – used for the elemental analysis or low contrast specimens.


Q 13. How can we use distance measurements tools of this system and what information we can get?

Ans: We can see the surface morphology, shape and size of the specimen. To find the exact particle size of the sample we used distance measurement tool, it can quickly measure the distance between two points of interest by dragging the mouse. We can also add simple graphics and comments to the image with the help of this tool.


Q 14. What are the variety of features equipped with the TM3000 software and how can we use all of them?

Ans: The TM3000 software is equipped with a variety of features to make it easy to use. First is Auto Focus which allow accurate focusing without the need of operator skills. Second is Auto Brightness which allows instant adjustment to brightness and contrast of the samples. Manual focusing and adjustment of brightness and contrast are also possible by just clicking “+ or –“buttons. Third but not the least Magnification adjustment can also be made by just simply dragging the mouse to quickly achieve to the required magnification. It can vary from 15x to 30,000x. An image shift function has also been used to image adjustment to high magnification by double-clicking on a point of interest at high magnification to move it to the centre of the screen. The TM3000 features the present magnification to save the frequently used magnification in the memory. The magnification can be changed to present value with a click of the mouse..


Q 15. What is the magnification range of this system?

Ans: The magnification range is varying from 15x to 30,000X.


Q 16. What kind of vacuum pumps are used with this system?

Ans: The TM3000 features a dry (oil-free) vacuum system consisting of a diaphragm pump for rough evacuation and high-performance turbo-molecular pump for the main pumping.


Q 17. What is the stage movement range and how can we move the stage inside the vacuum chamber?

Ans: The stage movement range is ±17.5 mm x ±17.5 mm and we can move the stage in the horizontal and vertical directions manually.


Q 18. What are the software’s used for SEM-EDAX system?

Ans: The software used for SEM is TM3000 software and the software used for EDAX is SWIFTED3000 software.


Q 19. What is the role of detector control and how can we operate it?

Ans: The role of the detector control is to make the system in either a cool or warm state. The operational state of the detector can be viewed and controlled by selecting detector control from the options menu in the SWIFTED3000 software. To change the operational state of the detector, select between “standby” and “operate”. When standby is selected, the detector warms up. When operate is selected, the detector cools down.


Q 20. How many view modes are present in SWIFTED3000 software?

Ans: The SWIFTED3000 software is equipped with a variety of view modes to make it easy to use.
(1) Spectrum view mode
(2) Image view mode
(3) Point &ID scan acquisition mode
(4) Elemental map view mode
(5) Line scan view mode.


Q 21. Explain each view modes present in SWIFTED3000 software?

Ans: The different view modes are as follows:

(1) Spectrum view mode- It acquires and displays the x-ray spectrum for the region currently defined by the microscope. Use for elemental identification and quantification.

(2) Image view- It acquires and displays the electron image for the region currently defined by the microscope

(3) Point &ID scan acquisition- It acquires and displays x-rays spectra from discrete points or an area of interest within the region currently defined by the microscope. There are two modes of operation:

(a) Point mode- select one or more points by clicking directly on the electron image. SWIFTED3000 acquires spectra from each point in.

(b) Rectangle mode- select an area of interest by drawing a rectangle on the electron image.

(4) Elemental map view- It acquires and displays the distribution for each element present. In addition, 3 elements can be displayed simultaneously in RGB, overlaid upon the BSC image.

(5) Line scan view- It acquires and displays the x-ray spectrum from a line between two fixed points in the region currently defined by the microscope.


Q 22. Which form of samples can be used in SEM-EDAX system?

Ans: The samples can be used in pellet form, thin film mode and samples in powder form.


Q 23. Does this system have a liquid sample assembly?

Ans: No


Q 24. What are the strengths of EDAX system?

Ans: The strengths of EDAX system are:
Quickly observe compositional analysis
Versatile, inexpensive, and widely available
Quantitative for some samples (flat, polished, homogeneous)


Q 25. What are the limitations of EDAX system?

Ans: The limitations of EDAX system are:
1. The semi-quantitative analysis is possible.
2. The sample size must be compatible with SEM.
3. Samples must be vacuum compatible.
4. Analysis (and coating) may restrict the subsequent surface analysis.
5. Numerous elemental peak overlaps are possible, a careful review of the spectra is required.


Q 26. What are the main components of the SEM system?

Ans: The main components of the SEM system are electron source, condenser lenses, sample stage, detectors (Backscattered, Secondary) and display/data output devices.


Q 27. Which X-ray detector is used for this system?

Ans: For X-ray detection, silicon drift detector (SSD) is used.


Q 28. Can we see the cross-section view of the thin film samples?

Ans: Yes, we can see the cross-section image of the thin film samples.


Q 29. Does this instrument requires liquid nitrogen (LN2) to cool down the system?

Ans: No


Q 30. What version software does TM3000 SEM required?

Ans: This SEM system run on either windows 7 or windows 10.


Q 31. Is specimen coating required with the TM3000’s charge up reduction mode?

Ans: No need for specimen coating required with the TM3000’s charge up reduction mode.


Q 32. Is it possible to do EDAX for polymer or fabric samples without coating?

Ans: Yes, it is possible to do EDAX for polymer or fabric samples without coating by using lower acceleration voltage (i.e. 5KV).


Q 33. What is the recommended working distance between the sample and the beam for the EDAX analysis and EDAX mapping measurements?

Ans: The recommended working distance is 8.5 between the sample and the beam for EDAX analysis and EDAX mapping measurements.


Q 34. Can we do SEM-EDAX measurements of a liquid samples?

Ans: No, we cannot do measurements of a liquid samples.
